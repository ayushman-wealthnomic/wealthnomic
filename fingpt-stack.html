INITIATIVE

FinGPT Stack

Built on the leading FinGPT framework. We provide domain-adapted LLMs optimized for institutional research, earnings call parsing, and compliance analysis.

The Thesis

Language is the leading indicator of market sentiment. Generic LLMs are trained on general internet data and often miss the critical nuances of financial communications—FOMC guidance, earnings calls, and regulatory shift alerts.

FinGPT Stack is our production-ready implementation of the FinGPT project philosophy. We take state-of-the-art models like Llama-3 and Mistral and fine-tune them on hundreds of billions of financial tokens.

With a 128K context window and RAG-native architecture, the FinGPT Stack can "read" an entire company's historical filings in seconds, identifying contradictions and extraction signals with 94.2% accuracy vs. human analyst consensus.

Capabilities

Sentiment Benchmarking FOMC Reasoning Earnings Call Q&A RAG Pipelines Compliance Monitoring Named Entity Recognition Synthetic Data Gen

1. Domain-Adapted Fine-Tuning

Our models use Instruction Tuning (SFT) and Reinforcement Learning from Real-world Feedback (RLRF) to minimize hallucination in financial reports. We focus on numerical reasoning and entity relationship mapping.

2. RAG-Native Architecture

Integration with vector databases allows for Retrieval-Augmented Generation over live news feeds and SEC archives. The stack doesn't just "remember"—it retrieves and cites.

3. High-Context Reasoning

Support for 128K+ tokens means you can feed in dozens of quarterly reports simultaneously for multi-period trend analysis without losing context or resolving to crude summarization.

Sample Inference (Python)

# Analyze FOMC Minutes for Hawkish/Dovish tilt from fingpt_stack import FinGPT model = FinGPT.from_pretrained("wealthnomics/fingpt-llama3-70b") result = model.analyze_document( path="fomc_minutes_q4.txt", task="sentiment_tilt", rag_enabled=True ) print(f"Sentiment: {result.label} | Confidence: {result.score}") print(f"Key Reasoning: {result.explanations[0]}")
